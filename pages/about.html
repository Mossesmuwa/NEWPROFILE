I'm Ivan — an IT professional building a serious career in cybersecurity. I work in IT which means I understand how systems actually work before trying to break them. That's not a small thing — most attackers exploit gaps that defenders never thought to look for because they've never had to build the system.

My approach is simple: no theory without practice. Every concept I study, I build a lab for. Every tool I learn, I use on real (intentionally vulnerable) systems until I understand it properly — not just how to run the command, but why it works.

I'm currently learning German (Deutsch) — targeting B2 for professional opportunities. Languages and security have something in common: both reward precision and punish assumptions.

Philosophy

How I think

"Security isn't a product. It's a habit."
You can't buy your way to being secure. Every firewall, every certificate, every tool is only as good as the daily discipline behind it.
"The best way to defend a system is to know how to break it first."
Defenders who've never attacked don't know what they're missing. I learn offensive skills so my defensive instincts are grounded in reality.
"Most breaches aren't clever. They're patient."
Attackers don't need to be geniuses. They just need to find one unpatched system, one reused password, one human moment. That's where the real work is.
